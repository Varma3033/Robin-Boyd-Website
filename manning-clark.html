MANNING CLARK HOUSE

The house is sited along the high side of a north sloping block, taking advantage of the north aspect and outlook to the well laid out garden.

The house is of simple masonry construction, with bagged walls painted light grey with charcoal trim, timber framed windows and metal deck roof.

Two separate parallel wings divide the living and sleeping areas, which are connected by a glass walled passage and entrance. Either side of this passage is a courtyard, one facing north and the other (the main entrance) facing south.

Information courtesy of: Australian Institute of Architects RSTCA Citation No. R108
